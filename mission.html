Industry Inflection
Big data. IoT. Software-Defined Networking.

These important and disruptive technologies prove an undeniable fact: we are at an inflection point in many of our technology industries. Networks are changing — the need for data, security and visibility is at an all time high. Hardware is transitioning — we’re looking for ways to inject as much intelligence as possible into devices, while simultaneously reducing operational and maintenance costs. Collaboration is essential — but it requires encryption and accessibility.

Overall, it boils down to the fact that the global community is headed toward cost-effective and well-developed software. It isn’t enough to have data passing constantly through our servers; we need to be able to understand it using Telemetry. It isn’t enough to be able to pass packets through our DCs and WANs; we want to be able to route traffic using Segment Routing. Where we can, we want to use Automation to simplify our packages and deployments by limiting human interaction.
